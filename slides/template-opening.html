Welcome

Welcome everyone to the first DANES Summer School and Hackathon. This week we bring together 35 participants from 15 countries to learn, experiment, and build together at the intersection of digital methods and the ancient world.

What is DANES ScHack?

A summer school

Five days of intensive, hands-on teaching in three parallel tracks — combining morning theory sessions with afternoon hackathon work on your own data.

A hackathon

Each afternoon you work in small groups, applying what you learned in the morning to real research questions and datasets. On Friday, you present your findings.

Part of the DANES Network

Digital Ancient Near Eastern Studies is an international network fostering digital and computational approaches to the ancient Near East and neighbouring traditions.

How the Hackathon Works

The idea

Each afternoon session is a structured work sprint in small groups. You apply the morning's methods to your own data — or to shared datasets provided by the teachers.

Your groups

Groups of 3–4 participants are arranged by method interest and complementary skills, not by track alone. Each group has a natural technical lead.

What you produce

  • A working notebook or pipeline demonstrating your method
  • Preliminary results — or honest documentation of what didn't work
  • A 10-minute presentation on Friday afternoon

No pressure to finish. The point is the process — what you tried, what you learned, and what you'd do next.
